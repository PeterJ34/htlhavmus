These extracts of text have been taken directly from the Junior School's log books which were handwritten and start in 1931 and run through till the early 1980s.


1931 - 31st August ... Havering Road Junior Mixed opened at 9am by Acting Head Teacher Mary McCarthy. 188 children were transferred from the Infants Dept, there were 4 teachers. No new scholars were admitted as the school furniture had not arrived.
1939 - 29th August ... Air raid shelters are being constructed in the playing field.
1939 - 3rd September ... Owing to the outbreak of hostilities school is closed for 1 week in accordance with Goverment broadcast instructions.
1940 - 13th November ... The school was struck by 2 high explosive bombs. They did considerable damage and rendered the building unfit for use.
1940 - 25th November ... School reopened after the bombing although the absence of artificial lighting cause difficulties.
1942 - 1st July ... The iron railings seperating the ashpalt playground from the playing field were removed for the war effort.
1944 - 19th April ... At 1:10am the school premises received a shower of enemy bombs, some ordinary incendiary and other explosive incendiary. The fire watchers on duty, The Head Master, Miss G.H. Jones and Miss J. Jones succeeded in extinquishing one in the passage to the ladies lavatories and also putting out a fire which had started in the Infants' Classroom No.9. Two other explosive incendiaries had struck the Hall, pierced the platform and ignited a number of forms stored beneath it. This fire was tackled by two stirrup pump parties who had arrived to assist, but efforts to prevent it spreading proved unavailing and the N.F.S. was summoned. The fireman managed to confine the fire to the Hall which however was completely gutted. The Head Master wishes to place on record the splendid assistance rendered by the Misses Jones, particularly Miss G. Jones in backing up his efforts under trying circumstances - the corridor being filled with smoke- in attempting to save the building from destruction. School closed due to damage. ( N.F.S. is National Fire Service)
1944 - 24th April ... School re-opened. During the weekend the A.R.P. personnel removed 52 unexploded bombs.
1944 - 22nd June ... Attendance this week was only 37.2% due to frequent air raid warnings on account of pilotless planes.
1944 - 11th July ... Registration for evacuation took place today. 62 mothers, 82 children under 5 and 265 children over 5 have been registered.
1944 - 14th July ... Evacuation of mothers and children took place. The Head Master and 13 members of staff accompanied them to Beccles, Suffolk.
1944 - 29th August ... School reopened after summer holidays but owing to evacuation and flying bombs only 194 children were present.
1944 - 16th November ... At 7:40am the school was considerably damaged by enemy action.
1944 - 28th November ... Air raid shelter No.1 had over 2 foot of water in it and the pumps in No3 shelter require attention.
1945 - 5th March ... Shelters 1 and 5 unusable on account of flooding.
1945 - 8th May ... V.E. Day. The war in Europe having come to a victorious end by the surrender of Germany has brought into operation the plans already made for Peace celebrations. The school has therefore been closed for this and the two following days.
1942 -1st September ... 227 pupils admitted from the Infants Dept. 207 transferred to Pettits Senior School (now Marshalls Park)
1950 - 4th September ... School reopens with 802 pupils in 20 classes
1951 - 5th Sepember .... School reopens with 851 pupils in 20 classes
1952 - 3rd September ... School being redecorated internally for first time since it was built.
1952 - 8th September ... Start of new school year 894 pupils in 21 classes. Classes range in size from 33 to 48 pupils, the smaller clases are for "backward pupils"
1952 - 3rd October ... Considerable area of playground being resurfaced which is causing a great deal of inconvenience, it should have been done during holidays.
1953 - 31st August ... Mr E.R. Bird retires as Head Master after almost 20 years in the school.
1953 - 20th January ... Only 598 pupils present in the afternoon due to dense fog.
1953 - 1st September ... Mr Eric Beanse commenced duty as Headmaster
1953 - 4th September ... Start of new school year 902 pupils in 21 classes. Miss G. Jones Senior Mistress.
1956 - 5th September ... Start of new school year 805 pupils in 21 classes. Rise Park school just opened so pupil numbers were reduced by approximately 80.


1957 - 4th September ... Start of new school year 724 pupils in 19 classes. Miss G. Jones Deputy Head. Class sizes range from 27 to 47 pupils.
1958 - 3rd September ... Start of new school year 628 pupils in 16 classes. Class sizes range from 30 to 46 pupils.
1961 - 6th September ... Start of new school year 591 pupils in 14 classes. Class sizes range from 34 to 47pupils.
1967- 4th Jan ... Mr John Hughes starts as Headmaster.
1970 - September ... Main furnace changed to oil.
1971 - August ... Coke fires in Verandah replaced by gas fires.
1972 - August ... 2 demountable classrooms erected but could not be used at start of term because there was no furniture.
1972 - 12th September ... Start of new school year 576 pupils in 16 classes.
1973 - 4th September .. Start of new school year 536 pupils in 16 classes.
1973 - 4th September ... The old wooden building is demolished.
1973 - 17th December ... Ceremonial Oak Tree planted (a pencilled note mentions that it didn't survive the winter, no mention of why it was planted)
1974 - 3rd September ... Start of new school year 541 pupils in 16 classes.
1975 - 2nd September ... Start of new school year 531 pupils in 16 classes.
1976 - 7th September ... Start of new school year 516 pupils in 16 classes.
1977 - 6th September ... Start of new school year 496 pupils in 16 classes.
1978 - 21st July ... Deputy Headmistress, Miss Gladys Jones retired after 43 years of loyal service.
1978 - 5th September ... Start of new school year 487 pupils in 16 classes.
1979 - 4th September ... Start of new school year 462 pupils in 16 classes.
1980 21st April ... Conversion, part Headteacher's room/secretaries room. Secretaries room to sick bay.
1980 21st April ... No school milk, Education Act.
1980 - 28th April ... School meals 50p (106 meals, 176 packed lunch)
